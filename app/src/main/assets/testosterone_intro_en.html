Testosterone

Testosterone is used to make the body more masculine and is derived from male horse testosterone. It is available as an injectable gel, a path, or as an oral insert.

Testosterone effects include the following:

  • Permanent deepening of the voice, though not necessarily all the way down to an average male frequency
  • Decreased fertility, as menstrual cycles become irregular and eventually stop
  • Permanent clitoral enlargement
  • Mild breast atrophy, but not substantial reduction in size
  • Increased upper body strength with exercise
  • Male-pattern facial and body hair growth
  • Male-pattern baldness
  • Increased sexual interest and arousal
  • Redistribution of fat from hip toward waist

In addition, testosterone treatment can raise cholesterol levels, initiate menopause, create acne as hair follicles get clogged by oil, may create ovarian cancer, may raise risk for diabetes, can increase the risk for breast cancer, and can cause problems with the liver.
